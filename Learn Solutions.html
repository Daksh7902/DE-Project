Hardware threats can be physical or low-level attacks. These simple steps help protect your devices and data from tampering, damage, or theft.

1. Physically Secure Devices

Lock laptops with cable locks, keep devices in a secure place, and never leave them unattended.

Learn more ‚Üí

2. Use Strong BIOS/UEFI Passwords

Set a BIOS/UEFI password to prevent unauthorized changes to startup settings or boot order.

Learn more ‚Üí

3. Keep Firmware Updated

Update device firmware (router, SSD, BIOS) from official vendors to patch low-level vulnerabilities.

Learn more ‚Üí

4. Avoid Unknown USB Devices

Do not plug in unknown USB drives or devices. Use trusted USBs and scan them before use.

Learn more ‚Üí

5. Use Hardware Encryption

Enable built-in disk encryption (e.g., BitLocker, FileVault) to protect data if a device is stolen.

Learn more ‚Üí

6. Protect Ports & Peripherals

Disable unused ports in BIOS or OS and avoid connecting unknown peripherals to your machine.

Learn more ‚Üí

7. Monitor for Tampering

Regularly inspect devices for physical tampering (opened cases, loose screws, or extra hardware).

Learn more ‚Üí

8. Use Surge Protectors & UPS

Protect hardware from power surges and keep an uninterruptible power supply (UPS) for essential devices.
